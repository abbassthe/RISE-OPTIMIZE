The dataset simulates insect observations from January 2023 to August 2024. It includes 16 species of insects commonly found in various habitats:

Apis mellifera (European honey bee)
Bombus terrestris (Buff-tailed bumblebee)
Vespula vulgaris (Common wasp)
Pieris rapae (Small white butterfly)
Aglais io (European peacock butterfly)
Vanessa atalanta (Red admiral butterfly)
Gonepteryx rhamni (Common brimstone butterfly)
Lasius niger (Black garden ant)
Formica rufa (Red wood ant)
Myrmica rubra (European fire ant)
Xylocopa violacea (Violet carpenter bee)
Osmia bicornis (Red mason bee)
Vespa crabro (European hornet)
Polistes dominula (European paper wasp)
Papilio machaon (Old World swallowtail butterfly)
Coccinella septempunctata (Seven-spot ladybird)

The data represents co-occurrences of these species across a geographic area (longitudes -5 to 5, latitudes 50 to 55) over time.
